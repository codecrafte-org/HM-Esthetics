Common Queries

Is it the same as Acupuncture?

No. While both use thin needles, Acupuncture is based on ancient energy flow. Dry Needling is based on modern Western medicine and focuses specifically on releasing painful muscle knots and improving blood flow to injured tissues.

Does it hurt?

The needles are so thin that you often don't feel them going in. When the needle hits the "knot," you might feel a quick muscle twitch or a dull ache for a second—this is actually a good sign that the muscle is finally relaxing!

How many sessions will I need?

Most patients feel relief after just 1 or 2 sessions. However, for long-standing chronic pain, a course of 3 to 5 sessions is usually recommended to fully "reset" the muscles.

Are there any side effects?

Your muscles might feel a bit tired or "sore" for 12-24 hours, similar to how you feel after a hard workout. Drinking plenty of water and light stretching helps this pass quickly.
